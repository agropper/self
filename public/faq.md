# Frequently Asked Questions

<details>
<summary>How is MAIA different from ChatGPT Health, Claude Health, Microsoft Copilot Health, Amazon Health or Perplexity Health?</summary>

- MAIA does not ask you to share private information with some AI you don't choose or control.
- MAIA is open source and available for security and privacy analysis at the GitHub. You can ask any frontier AI like Claude Code to review the MAIA code or check social media before you use it.
- MAIA and your private records are hosted separately from the MAIA code. This demonstration runs at Digital Ocean on my credit card. If you pay for your own hosting at Digital Ocean or an equivalent data center, I will have no access to your data.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Why is open source important?</summary>

- As AI agents become more powerful, it's important to keep them under your control.
- Open source software allow you and any community of users to analyze and even modify MAIA. Users and communities are encouraged to discuss any problems, suggest improvements, and can even create alternatives if the MAIA community is not responsive to their needs.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What are all the files MAIA adds to my health records folder?</summary>

- These files back up your MAIA so you can avoid keeping private files in the cloud any longer than necessary. This also saves money.
- The log files are useful if you need support. If you make sure your name is not in the filenames, the logs will not disclose any private information.
- If you want more info about these files, point Claude Code to the MAIA source code repository (see About) and ask.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Is MAIA regulated by FDA or anyone else?</summary>

- MAIA is decision support software which is explicitly outside FDA regulation:
  - 21st Century Cures Act, Pub. L. No. 114-255, §3060(a), 130 Stat. 1033 (2016) (codified at 21 U.S.C. §360j(o)).
  - U.S. Food and Drug Administration. Clinical Decision Support Software: Guidance for Industry and Food and Drug Administration Staff. January 6, 2026.
- State-level regulation of AI has been discussed but we are not aware of any as of April 2026. Physician practice is subject to state regulation but we are not aware any medical practice regulation that would apply to AI or MAIA as of April 2026.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can my physician use MAIA?</summary>

- Yes. When you share a deep link to a chat, your physician is using MAIA with your consent and there is no FDA or other restriction.
- Your doctor can also suggest MAIA to you or run a MAIA for you.

</details>

</details>

<details>
<summary>How much will MAIA hosting cost at DigitalOcean?</summary>

- MAIA hosting costs about $35/month would cover up to a dozen family and friends as long as they are not concerned about the account owner invading their privacy.
- A single user or small group can run MAIA as needed for about $1/day by destroying the cloud resources when the chats and deep-links are inactive. The records, chats, and deep links are backed up on the user's computer and can be restored to their cloud account or a new cloud account in less than 15 minutes for 1,000 pages of records.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I host MAIA on my own computer instead of the cloud?</summary>

- Maybe, but it's not practical. Even with a computer costing $thousands, performance would be abysmal and technical knowledge needed for privacy and security would be significant.

</details>
